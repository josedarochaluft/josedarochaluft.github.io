Windborne Project Details

In this project, I used the visual scripting tools available in Unreal Engine 4 to quickly prototype, test and implement game mechanics. Some of my contributions were:

Track editing tool, facilitating faster and more efficient iterations to level design.

Segmented vehicle destruction and repair, allowing the player to survive crashes that only destroy part of the glider.

Time manipulation, enabling the player to slow down time to traverse difficult obstacles. Also slowing down crashes for dramatic effect.

Diegetic energy meter. Energy is represented as the glow of the glider's crystal, rather than in a HUD.

During the game's production, I did extensive testing of game mechanics and took part in level design. Improving object placement and fixing issues.

Near the end of production, I also made this trailer. A download link for Windborne can be found in the video's description.

Portotype Project Details

Because there is a big difference in how many inputs a player can give between PC and Android, I decided that a turn-based game would be ideal because input would be simple but it could support complex game mechanics.

After achieving a basic random terrain generation and finishing the core of the combat system, I spent most of the time making sure the game ran perfectly across all platforms.

I wanted the terrain to look more natural, so the last feature I was working on was to make the terrain become more like a slope on a hill. Unfortunately, I had to move on to other projects and this feature was left out.

I still plan to expand upon this project in the future. This time with a priority in the game itself rather than porting.

Pac Monster Project Details

Pac Monster is my take on the arcade classic: Pac-Man. However, instead of just cloning the gameplay from Pac-Man I decided to make a deconstructed version of it instead. Inverting some concepts of the original game while retaining others.

Instead of controlling Pac-Man who is outnumbered by the ghosts who chase him, the player controls a ghost that is outnumbered by Pac-Minis - miniature clones of Pac-Man.

If a Pac-Mini touches a Power Pellet, it will grow to become a Pac-Monster and chase the ghost in an attempt to eat it.

The player can use his dashing ability to avoid Pac-Monsters, this is crucial to survival as the game gets harder over time.
